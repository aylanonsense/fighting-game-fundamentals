What is a fighting game?

A video game in which two players each control a character in a 2D plane. Players win by reducing their opponent to zero health through damaging moves.

Strategy

Strategy

There are three big situations you should understand:

  • Defending yourself
  • Attacking your opponent
  • The "neutral game"

Defending yourself

Situation: The opponent is hitting you! :'(

Goal: block until you find an opening

Defending yourself

Whenever you get hit (blocked or not), you slide a bit

They can't keep attacking forever!

Defending yourself

Low attacks can only be blocked while crouching, overhead attacks can only be blocked while standing

Defending yourself

A throw is type of attack where your opponent grabs and tosses you. Throws are unblockable

Defending yourself

Throws have very short range. If the opponent moves in close for a throw, don't block, hit them!

Defending yourself

The fundamental rock-paper-scissors of fighting games:

Defending yourself

If you fail to block, your opponent can combo you (hit you with multiple attacks) and there's nothing you can do

All you can do is avoid getting hit in the first place

Defending yourself

  • Just keep blocking
  • Block low/overhead attaks by crouching/standing
  • Hit em if they try to throw you
  • If you fail to block they can combo you—don't sweat it

Attacking your opponent

Attacking your opponent

Situation: you are hitting your opponent! :D

Goal: get past their defenses and deal damage

Attacking your opponent

When your opponent blocks an attack they enter blockstun for a couple of frames, during which they can't do anything other than block

That means they can't retaliate!

Attacking your opponent

This attack inflicts 20 frames of blockstun

After hitting, it recovers in 9 + 7 = 16 frames

If blocked, you can start another attack 20 - 16 = 4 frames before your opponent—this is called frame advantage

Attacking your opponent

This attack inflicts 20 frames of blockstun

After hitting, it recovers in 6 + 21 = 27 frames

If blocked, you can start another attack 20 - 27 = -7 frames after your opponent—this is called frame disadvantage

Attacking your opponent

An attack that gives frame advantage is "safe on block"

That means that the opponent can't retaliate, you can keep attacking, and they have to keep blocking!

As long as your opponent is blocking, you want to stick to moves that are safe on block

Attacking your opponent

Remember to mix low attacks, overhead attacks, and throws into your assault to catch your opponent off guard

A situation where the opponent is forced to guess how to defend is called a mix up

Attacking your opponent

"Tripping" attacks will knock down your opponent, they then have to go through a standing animation

This is the perfect time to set up a mix up and put them in another bad situation, called okizeme ("waking attack")

Attacking your opponent

Your opponent is blocking by holding away from you

If you jump to the other side of them, that direction changes. This type of mix up called a cross up

Attacking your opponent

When your opponent fails to block, now's your chance!

A hit confirm is when you notice the opponent failed to block and turn your single hit into a combo!

Attacking your opponent

A combo is a string of attacks that are guaranteed to hit provided the first attack is unblocked

Attacking your opponent

  • Use attacks that are safe on block
  • Mix in low attacks, overhead attacks, and throws
  • When they fail to block, combo them!

The neutral game

The neutral game

Situation: neither player has an immediate advantage

Goal: gain an advantage for yourself

The neutral game

The neutral game is extremely complex because both players have all options open to them

So what do you do?

The neutral game

Properly spacing yourself is CRITICAL

At long range none of your attacks will reach each other

At short range all of them will

The neutral game

In-between those two extremes interesting stuff happens

The neutral game

Cammy (left) has a long-range kick that outranges all of Guile's (right) normal moves

She might try to abuse this by staying right at that range

The neutral game

If Guile moves just outside of that range, he can bait her into using an attack that will whiff (miss)

The neutral game

Because of the extended hurtbox from her attack, Guile can counterattack and punish from where he is

The neutral game

Cammy might respond by moving extra close to make sure Guile can't get away, but then he is in range to hit her

This back-and-forth of spacing is called footsies

The neutral game

If you are able to dominate the footsies game you can start zoning your opponent (deciding where they stand)

In most fighting games there is a character built specifically for this aspect of the game, called zoners

The neutral game

Zoners rely on pokes to deal damage and control space

These moves will never combo, but they make it incredibly hard for the opponent to get anywhere close to retaliate

Most characters have a couple of good pokes

The neutral game

Projectiles are a particularly potent at zoning

They move independent of your character

They cover a lot of ground and meanwhile you can take other actions to zone your opponent (like more projectiles!)

The neutral game

Moves that are good at zoning are usually bad at short range

Instead of zoning your opponent, you can just try to get up close and personal as fast as possible

Again, most fighting games have characters who specialize in closing the distance as fast as possible and staying there, called rushdown characters

The neutral game

Because both players have everything at their disposal, the neutral game is varied and matchup-dependent

In general:

  • Find the optimal distance from your opponent
  • Punish your opponent's whiffed moves
  • Poke from range
  • Close the distance if it's advantageous
